## The Webizen Charter

### Preamble

**Whereas** the inherent dignity and the equal and inalienable rights of all members of the human family constitute the foundation of freedom, justice, and peace, as articulated in the Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR), the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights (ICCPR), and related international human rights instruments;

**Whereas** the digital ecosystem, the internet, and the Semantic Web do not exist as separate jurisdictions or extralegal frontiers, but rather function as a primary modality through which human beings exercise their fundamental rights to freedom of expression, peaceful assembly, economic participation, and the pursuit of knowledge;

**Recognizing** that the human actor within this digital modality—the *Webizen*—must retain the inviolable right to self-determination, autonomy, and the absolute protection of their personal data, cognitive outputs, and collaborative efforts against unwarranted surveillance, corporate enclosure, or systemic abstraction;

**Noting with grave concern** the emergence of centralized, proprietary cognitive infrastructures that seek to mediate, absorb, and claim ownership over human intellect; systems that operate under a false dichotomy of absolute vendor control, thereby threatening global stability, socio-economic resilience, and diplomacy by imposing an architecture of compulsory dependence;

**Reaffirming** categorically that algorithms, generative models, inference engines, and all forms of software agents are strictly mechanical and mathematical instruments; that they are fundamentally devoid of legal personhood, moral agency, or the capacity to hold or transfer intellectual property; and that they exist solely as utilitarian mechanisms for the actuation of human intent;

**Acknowledging** that to protect human rights in a computationally advanced society, the provenance of digital actions must be transparent and mathematically verifiable, necessitating the absolute decoupling of human authorship from machine execution;

**Determining** that the realization of human-centric architecture requires the abandonment of centralized identity provisioning in favor of an enumerated state—defined strictly by the use of multiple cryptography-supported identifiers and related datasets on an agent- and entity-centric basis—to securely anchor the rights-bearing human to their digital expressions;

**Now, Therefore,** this Webizen Charter is proclaimed as a foundational standard of architecture and protocol design for all human-centric technological systems, to the end that every individual, software developer, and network node shall strive to encode these principles into the foundational layers of the web. This Charter mandates that the digital substrate must permanently uphold human dignity, enforce transparent collaboration, and secure the mathematical verification of human agency through the precise, auditable tracking of machine actuation.
